Green Finance, Government and Carbon Emission

With China's carbon peaking and carbon neutrality goals coming up, the problem over how to reduce carbon emission and grow more sustainably is increasingly importaant. To find out whether green finance is beneficial for reducing the carbon and how the government works, we uses the data of 285 cities in China from 2013 to 2017, exploring the impact of green financial development on carbon emission through panel data model. Based on the different model settings and variable measures, we control the endogeneity of the related variables.

What Factors influence Migrant Workers' Medical Welfare

Migrant workers in China often suffer from many difficulties when going to the hospital for reimbursement of medical expenses because of household registration. To solve these problems the Chinese government has introduced many policies for migrant workers' medical treatment recent years. In order to see whether workers' welfare has been improved and what factors matter most, we design a questionnaire and a model to analyse the issue, and also interview some doctors in Beijing to figure out the effectiveness of policy implementation.

Shaow Empire: the Collapse of Xiao Jianhua's Financial Empire

The bankruptcy of Baoshang Bank shocks China for it has been over 20 years since a bank goes bankrupt. Behind the financial disaster, people find some shareholders kept undermining the bank which turns out to be a member of "Tomorrow Group", one of the largest private financial groups in China. The government realized the Tomorrow Group and its founder Xiao Jianhua had increased China's financial systemic risk and finally dismantled Xiao's financial empire.
